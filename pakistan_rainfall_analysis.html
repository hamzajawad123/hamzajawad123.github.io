Rainfall Analysis

Comprehensive Rainfall Metrics and Insights

Introduction

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of annual and monthly rainfall patterns across different cities in Pakistan. It highlights key metrics such as total rainfall, rainfall intensity, and rainy days. The analysis aims to understand regional rainfall distribution for improved water resource management and climate monitoring.

Objective

The objective is to evaluate rainfall behavior throughout the year, identify the wettest months, and analyze rainfall across major cities. These insights support planning in agriculture, water storage, and urban infrastructure management.

Key Metrics Summary

The analysis revealed a total annual rainfall of 12,840 mm with an average intensity of 356.7 mm per rainfall event. The maximum daily rainfall recorded was 268 mm, and there were on average 36 rainy days annually. These indicators highlight the country's varying climate conditions and the uneven distribution of rainfall throughout the year.

Rainfall Trends by Month

Monthly analysis showed significant variations in rainfall. The dry months such as January and February recorded relatively lower rainfall values of 35,393 mm and 45,567 mm respectively. Rainfall increased gradually during the spring months, peaking sharply in the monsoon period.

The wettest months were July with 96,204 mm and August with an exceptional 126,211 mm, marking the peak of monsoon season. Rainfall declined in the post-monsoon months such as October (51,339 mm) and reached its lowest levels in November (10,750 mm) and December (8,873 mm).

Insight: August had the highest rainfall among all months, confirming it as the most critical period for flood management and agricultural planning.

Top 10 Cities by Rainfall

The study identified the top 10 cities with the highest recorded rainfall. At the top of the list is Islamabad with 52,170 mm, followed by Khyber Pakhtunkhwa with 48,284 mm and Kohistan Upper at 40,854 mm. These cities experienced intense and consistent rainfall throughout the year.

Other notable cities include Mardan (36,217 mm), Attock (35,137 mm), and Gujrat Punjab (31,570 mm). Northern cities such as Chitral Upper and Kohat also reported moderate rainfall levels between 25,000â€“26,000 mm. The lowest among the top 10 was South Waziristan with 21,370 mm.

Observation: Rainfall concentration is highest in northern regions, particularly near mountainous areas, indicating topographic influence on precipitation patterns.

Conclusion

This analysis concludes that rainfall distribution in Pakistan is highly uneven, with monsoon months contributing the majority of annual precipitation. August stands out as the wettest month, while regions such as Islamabad and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa experience the heaviest rainfall overall. These findings emphasize the importance of regional forecasting and adaptive water management strategies to mitigate flood risks and optimize agricultural practices.

Recommendations

1. Strengthen early warning systems for monsoon floods, particularly in Islamabad and northern regions.
2. Enhance rainwater harvesting systems to utilize high seasonal rainfall effectively.
3. Support agricultural scheduling around peak rainfall months to optimize crop yield.
4. Improve urban drainage infrastructure in high-rainfall cities to prevent waterlogging.
