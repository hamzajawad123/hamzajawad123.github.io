HR Analysis

Comprehensive HR Data Metrics and Insights

Introduction

This project analyzes employee attrition and workforce demographics to identify patterns affecting staff retention. The data includes key metrics such as employee count, attrition rate, and detailed breakdowns by gender, age, department, and job satisfaction.

Objective

The goal is to generate actionable insights from employee attrition data to support HR and management decision-making. Analysis focuses on understanding attrition drivers by department, gender, and age, while applying Excel functions to process and summarize the data efficiently.

Key Metrics Summary

The dataset contains 1,470 employees with an attrition count of 237, leading to an attrition rate of 16.12%. The average employee age is 37 years. Excel functions like =COUNT() and =SUM() were used to calculate these totals.

Attrition Breakdown by Gender

Attrition is higher among male employees (63.3%) compared to females (36.7%). The =COUNTIF(range, "Male") and =COUNTIF(range, "Female") functions helped filter and count gender-specific attrition cases.

Department-wise Attrition

The R&D department shows the highest attrition at 56.12%, followed by Sales at 38.82%. Human Resources experienced the lowest attrition (5.06%). To calculate percentages, formulas like =B2/SUM(B$2:B$4) were applied.

Age Distribution of Employees

Employee ages range from 18 to 60+, with the largest groups between 21 and 25 years old. The =FREQUENCY() function was utilized to create age group histograms for distribution analysis.

Job Satisfaction Ratings

Job roles were evaluated with satisfaction ratings from 1 to 4. For example, Sales Executives had the highest count with 326 employees distributed across the ratings. The =SUMIFS() function helped aggregate ratings by role.

Education Field-wise Attrition

Life Sciences and Medical fields contributed most to attrition counts. Excel's =SUMIF() function was useful to sum attritions by education categories efficiently.

Attrition Rate by Gender Across Age Groups

Attrition rates vary significantly by age and gender, with highest rates in the 25-34 age group. This detailed breakdown was calculated using =COUNTIFS() to apply multiple criteria simultaneously.

Conclusion

The HR analysis highlights that employee attrition is most pronounced among younger employees aged 25â€“34, particularly in the R&D and Sales departments. Male employees have higher attrition rates compared to females, suggesting potential differences in job satisfaction or career expectations. Life Sciences and Medical fields also contribute significantly to overall turnover. Addressing these factors can improve workforce stability and enhance employee retention.

Recommendations

1. Implement targeted retention programs for younger employees, focusing on career growth and mentorship opportunities.
2. Conduct department-specific engagement surveys to identify root causes of attrition in R&D and Sales.
3. Introduce flexible work arrangements or benefits to balance gender-based attrition trends.
4. Provide continuous learning and development programs to boost satisfaction among employees in high-attrition education fields.
