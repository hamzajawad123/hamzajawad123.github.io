E-commerce Sales Analysis

Comprehensive Sales Insights and Market Performance Review (SQL-Based Project)

Introduction

This project analyzes e-commerce sales performance using SQL queries applied to a structured relational database. The dataset includes tables for orders, customers, regions, and products. The aim is to explore sales behavior, profit trends, and customer segmentation to guide business strategy and enhance profitability.

Objective

The objective of this project is to use SQL for data-driven decision making by identifying key performance metrics such as total sales, profit, customer demographics, and regional distribution. SQL queries with SELECT region, SUM(sales), SUM(profit) FROM orders GROUP BY region; clauses were used to derive insights from the sales data efficiently.

Sales Overview

The dataset contained over 10,000 transaction records across multiple regions. Aggregate functions like SELECT SUM(sales), SUM(profit) FROM orders; were used to calculate total sales and total profit. The COUNT() function identified total orders and customers.

Regional Sales Performance

SQLâ€™s GROUP BY was used to summarize sales and profit by region: SELECT region, SUM(sales), SUM(profit) FROM orders GROUP BY region; The results revealed that the West Region contributed the highest share of total revenue (38%), followed by East, Central, and South regions.

Category-wise Profitability

Product categories were analyzed using: SELECT category, SUM(sales), SUM(profit) FROM products GROUP BY category; This helped identify which categories contributed most to revenue. The Technology category performed best, while Furniture faced reduced margins due to discounting.

Discount vs Profit Relationship

The relationship between discount and profit was studied using SQL correlation logic: SELECT discount, AVG(profit) FROM orders GROUP BY discount; It was observed that higher discounts resulted in lower profit margins.

Customer Segment Analysis

Customer segmentation was evaluated using: SELECT segment, SUM(sales) AS total_sales FROM customers JOIN orders ON customers.customer_id = orders.customer_id GROUP BY segment; The Consumer segment accounted for 52% of total revenue.

Top 5 Products by Sales

The top products were identified using: SELECT product_name, SUM(sales) AS total_sales FROM products JOIN orders ON products.product_id = orders.product_id GROUP BY product_name ORDER BY total_sales DESC LIMIT 5; These products represented 15% of the total revenue.

Conclusion

The e-commerce sales analysis demonstrated how SQL can efficiently uncover key business insights from structured datasets. The results highlighted that the West region leads in sales, while the Technology category remains the most profitable. Discounting negatively affects profit margins, suggesting that excessive price reductions may harm profitability. Consumer segments dominate the customer base, providing an opportunity for targeted marketing strategies. Overall, SQL-based data exploration proved valuable for optimizing sales strategies and improving business decisions.

Recommendations

1. Optimize Discount Strategy: Avoid deep discounts on low-margin products to sustain profitability.
2. Boost Technology Category: Invest more in technology-related marketing and stock levels to maximize revenue.
3. Target Consumer Segment: Develop personalized offers and loyalty programs for the Consumer segment, which generates over half the revenue.
4. Regional Expansion: Improve logistics and promotional efforts in underperforming regions like the South to balance overall growth.

Summary

  • Tools Used: SQL (MySQL), Joins, Aggregations, Subqueries
  • Data: Online E-commerce Sales Database
  • Skills: Query Optimization, Data Cleaning, KPI Reporting
